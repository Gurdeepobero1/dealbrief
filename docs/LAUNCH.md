# DealBrief Launch Plan

## Phase 0 — Validation (Weeks 1–2, before writing more code)

Do NOT skip this phase. The CLI we have now is enough to validate.

**20 customer discovery calls** with the ICP:
- 8 founder-led SaaS sellers (1–20 person teams)
- 6 AEs/SDRs at Series A–B startups
- 4 agency owners selling $10K+ services
- 2 BD/partnership managers

**Script for every call** (keep to 30 min):
1. Walk me through how you prepped for your last important call
2. What did you look for? Where did you look?
3. What did you wish you had?
4. What did you find that turned out to be useless?
5. How long did you spend?
6. Would you pay $29/month for a tool that did this for you automatically?
7. What would kill it for you? (deal-breakers)

**What you're listening for:**
- Recurring pain patterns across 5+ calls → real signal
- "Would you pay" with specific objections → nuanced signal
- Enthusiasm without specific workflow → be skeptical

## Phase 1 — Closed beta (Weeks 3–6)

- 30 design partners from the 20 discovery calls + referrals
- Run the CLI for them manually for 2 weeks (you operate it, deliver briefs via email)
- **Goal**: 30 real briefs delivered, 15 real meetings where the brief was used, 5 written testimonials

This is the step most founders skip. If you skip it, your ProductHunt launch will flop because you won't have real stories.

## Phase 2 — Private beta (Weeks 7–10)

- Ship the API + web dashboard (prompts 04, 06)
- Onboard the 30 design partners to self-serve
- Collect usage metrics: briefs generated, openers copied, meetings booked from briefs
- Iterate the synthesis prompt weekly based on feedback

## Phase 3 — ProductHunt launch (Week 12)

### 4 weeks before launch

- Ship the Chrome extension (prompt 07) — the extension is your demo GIF
- Recruit a hunter with 5K+ PH followers in sales/productivity (don't self-hunt)
- Build 3 case studies from design partners (1 founder, 1 AE, 1 agency)
- Start building in public on LinkedIn — your GTM background is your credibility
- Build waitlist page at `dealbrief.io` with genuinely good copy

### 2 weeks before launch

- 60-second demo video: calendar invite → 24h later brief appears → opener copied → reply received
- Write launch-day assets: first comment, tagline, gallery images (5 screenshots)
- Line up 20 people to leave *substantive* first-2-hour comments (not "congrats!")
- Send sneak-peek to Pavilion, RevGenius, SaaSBoomi Slacks

### Launch day (Tuesday 12:01 AM PT)

- Hunter posts
- First comment from you (template below)
- Gallery: demo GIF as first asset
- Offer: 50% off Pro for first 3 months for PH users, code `PHHUNT50`
- Respond to every comment within 1 hour for the first 12 hours
- DO NOT buy upvotes. PH will shadow-delist you.

### First-comment template

> Hey 👋 I'm [name]. I spent years doing B2B sales — drones to PSUs, investor outreach, proposal-writing. The part I hated most wasn't rejection. It was the 5 minutes before every call frantically Googling the prospect hoping to find *something* useful.
>
> Every existing tool I tried either gave me too much raw data (and no insight), or felt creepy — scraping personal Instagram and presenting it as "intelligence."
>
> DealBrief is the tool I wanted. 24 hours before every meeting, a brief lands in your inbox: who they are professionally, what their company's doing right now, and three openers grounded in their own public words. Every claim has a source. We deliberately don't touch personal social, phone lookup, or anything they didn't publish themselves.
>
> Happy to answer anything about the product, the privacy stance, the tech (free-tier LLMs on Groq + a 3-stage pipeline), or why building for GTM people matters to me.

### Post-launch (Week 1)

- Whatever happens on PH, keep shipping
- Follow up with every user who signed up — personal email from you, not automated
- Weekly content: "What I learned from 500 sales calls" style — turn your real experience into demand

## Metrics to track

**Acquisition:**
- Waitlist signups
- Sign-up → first-brief completion rate
- PH votes, comments, signups attributed

**Activation:**
- % of users who connect calendar within 48h of signup
- % of users who receive their first brief within 72h
- % of users who copy an opener within 7 days

**Retention:**
- Weekly active (connected calendar + received brief in last 7 days)
- Month-2 retention
- Churn reasons (exit survey)

**Business:**
- Free → Pro conversion rate
- MRR
- Net revenue retention

## Kill criteria

Set these now so you know when to pivot:

- <10 waitlist signups in 2 weeks after launching landing page → positioning is wrong
- <5 design partners willing to use the manual-ops version → nobody wants this
- <20% free-to-paid at month 2 → pricing or product is off
- PH launch yields <500 upvotes with good execution → category is wrong, not product

Don't flinch from these. Founders who pretend red flags aren't real burn years.
